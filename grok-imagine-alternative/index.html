The Best Grok Imagine Alternatives for Image and Video

First, a quick disambiguation: this is about Grok Imagine — xAI’s image and video generation model — not Grok the chatbot. If you’re here, you’re generating pictures or clips and wondering what else is out there.

Worth saying up front, because a lot of “alternatives” lists imply otherwise: Grok Imagine is not hard to access or expensive. xAI keeps a free tier, offers it inside SuperGrok at $30/month, and — the part most roundups miss — exposes a genuinely cheap developer API at about $0.02 per image and $0.05 per second of video. So the honest reason to look elsewhere usually isn’t cost or a paywall; it’s that you want a different look, a specific capability (transparency, a particular style, longer clips, audio), or simply the freedom to compare models before you commit. This guide covers the strongest image and video alternatives — each with what it’s good at, whether it has an API, and a real price — so you can pick on merit.

xAI’s own developer docs, captured July 23, 2026 — the Imagine API is publicly available at $0.02/image and $0.05/second, so Grok Imagine isn’t locked behind a paywall.

How to choose a Grok Imagine alternative

Two questions narrow the field fast. Image or video? Grok Imagine does both, but the best alternatives specialize, so decide which output you actually need. Do you need an API? If you’re building, you want programmatic access and per-unit pricing, not a consumer subscription — which rules some options in and others (like Midjourney) out.

Best image alternatives (an AI image generator like Grok)

Nano Banana (Google Gemini image). The strongest all-rounder if you want prompt accuracy and readable text in images. It’s API-first and runs about $0.039 per image for the base model, more for the Pro tier — we break the family down in the Nano Banana price guide. Pick it when correctness and text matter more than a signature art style.

Imagen 4 (Google). Google’s dedicated image model, cheaper at the entry tier (~$0.02 per image for Imagen 4 Fast) and reliable from a first-party endpoint. A sensible default when you want Google-grade quality without Nano Banana’s premium tiers.

Flux (Black Forest Labs). The budget champion: Flux Schnell on Replicate runs about $0.003 per image, an order of magnitude below the others, and it’s fully API-accessible. Reach for it when volume and cost dominate and you don’t need a specific house look.

Qwen Image (Alibaba). A capable, low-cost image model at around $0.021 per image via an aggregator — a solid middle option between Flux’s rock-bottom price and Nano Banana’s polish, and strong on non-English text.

Midjourney. The one to name for pure aesthetic, but with a caveat that decides it for builders: Midjourney has no public API and is subscription-only, from $10/month per its plan comparison. It’s a fine Grok Imagine alternative if you’re generating by hand — and a non-starter if you need to automate.

(One name you’ll see in older lists to skip: DALL·E — OpenAI retired it in May 2026, so it’s no longer a live option. Ideogram is still around and good at typography, but its API pricing sits above Flux’s.)

Best video alternatives (an AI video generator like Grok)

For a quality-first ranking across the video field, our best AI video generators roundup goes deeper; here are the standouts as Grok Imagine alternatives.

Veo 3.1 (Google). The premium pick, and the closest match to Grok Imagine’s “text-and-video with audio” ambitions. Its cheapest tier, Veo 3.1 Lite, is ~$0.05 per second (720p) — the same ballpark as Grok’s own video rate — with the full lineup in our Veo 3.1 price breakdown. Choose it when output quality is the deliverable.

Kling. A strong, widely-used video model with excellent motion, available pay-as-you-go from around $0.05 per second. Good when you want cinematic movement without Veo’s top-tier price.

Seedance (ByteDance). One of the cheapest credible video options — from about $0.012 per second (aggregator rate) — and API-accessible. A smart default for high-volume or draft work where cost per clip matters.

Hailuo (MiniMax). Known for lively, coherent short clips at roughly $0.02 per second, with API access. A good middle ground between Seedance’s price and Veo’s polish. For ultra-cheap, low-res drafts, Alibaba’s Wan flash tier goes as low as ~$0.0008/second — a different quality class, but hard to beat on price.

Runway. The other name in premium AI video, strong on control and editing tools. It runs on its own credit system rather than simple per-second billing and doesn’t carry Kling on its developer API — worth a look for its Gen-family models if you want a polished, standalone platform. See Kling vs Runway for how its channels and pricing actually work.

FAQ

Is there a free alternative to Grok Imagine?
Grok Imagine itself has a free tier, so you may not need one. Among alternatives, most API models bill per image or per second with no free tier; Google’s AI Studio lets you experiment for free, and Flux Schnell is cheap enough (~$0.003/image) to feel nearly free at low volume.

What’s the best Grok Imagine alternative for video?
For quality, Veo 3.1; for value, Seedance ($0.012/second) or Hailuo ($0.02/second). All three offer API access, which Grok’s consumer plans don’t foreground.

What’s a good AI image generator like Grok?
Nano Banana for prompt accuracy and text, Imagen 4 for a cheaper Google option, Flux for the lowest cost, and Midjourney for pure aesthetic (if you don’t need an API).

Does Grok Imagine have an API?
Yes — xAI’s Imagine API bills about $0.02 per image and $0.05 per second of video, so “no API” isn’t a reason to leave. People switch mainly for a different style, capability, or the ability to compare models.

Can I use these alternatives without signing up for each one?
Yes — an aggregation layer lets you call many of them (and Grok Imagine) through one pay-as-you-go key, which is the easiest way to compare before committing.
